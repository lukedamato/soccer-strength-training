Intro to Strength Training


1. Focus on form first before worrying about anything else
especially as an athelete the last possible thing you want is for your training to specifically benefit
your performance in your sport takes you out of that sport because of injury. Try your best to feel the muscle
groups that the exercise is supposed to be working as you are doing the movements.


2. In the same token as number 2, do not focus on the weight that is on the bar. Focus on doing the exercise correctly and
slowly building your strength up over time. I doubt you expect yourself to be able to run 5 miles the first
time you go for a run, in the same idea you wont be benching 315 for reps the first time you bench press


3. Do not overexert yourself, if something feels off bailing out on your last rep is better than pulling
a muscle in your back. Keep your mindset longterm, especially in the off season.

About us

Matt, Mike and Luke have been involved in athletics in some capacity for their entire lives, for Matt and Mike specifically their sport of choice has been soccer. Growing up playing soccer there isn't as much of an emphasis on strength training as there is with cardio, even though strength is a key aspect of the game. I even noticed it when assistant coaching for a highschool varsity team fall of 2019, if someone out there is bigger and stronger than you, they have a huge advantage in many aspects of the game. This is where we wanted to come in and give you a way to enhance your game even more by giving you all the resources a beginner weight lifter needs all in one place. It's hard enough to motivate yourself in the offseason to work out, without learning a whole new set of skills and having to research for hours on the best things to do, so we did that for you. Our goal was to create a way to get athletes to get into the gym and feel comfortable that they know what and why they are doing what they're doing.
